  • Conlusion

    CPE232, also known as Managing Enterprise Servers, teaches aspiring system administrators various topics in different fields of system administration. It is a multifaceted process encompassing various key topics, such as understanding the DevOps culture, that is paramount to streamlining collaboration between development and operations teams and fostering a culture of continuous integration and delivery. The aspect of SSH key-based authentication that provides a secure and efficient means of accessing servers. The introduction to Ansible adds an orchestration layer to server management, allowing the automation of tasks. Creating roles in Ansible facilitates the organization of tasks into reusable components, enhancing scalability and maintainability. Managing servers extends beyond Ansible, involving the handling of files and the creation of roles, providing a comprehensive approach to server configuration.

    Monitoring, installation, and configuration tools are essential for maintaining server health and performance. Containerization, exemplified by Docker, becomes a focal point for efficient deployment, as demonstrated through building applications in a containerized environment. Cloud services, particularly OpenStack, which offers a scalable and flexible infrastructure, and the detailed installation guide for OpenStack components, including Keystone, Glance, Nova, Neutron, Horizon, and Cinder, underscore the breadth of server management capabilities. These topics helped me learn and acquire skills that can help me excel in the field of system administration.

    Overall, the course CPE232, Managing Enterprise Servers, is a course that can enhance the skills of a person in system administration, focusing on server and system management. The activities given to me were a big help, as they became my stepping stone to improving myself. The exams were also helpful because that is where I applied all the knowledge that I acquired from doing the activities.
